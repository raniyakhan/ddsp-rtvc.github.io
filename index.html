RT-VC: Real-Time Zero-Shot Voice Conversion with DDSP Articulatory Synthesis

Yisi Liu, John Wang, Hanjo Kim, Raniya Khan, Gopala Anumanchipalli
University of California, Berkeley
Paper arXiv Demo Video Code

Abstract

Streaming voice conversion has emerged as a pivotal technology in numerous applications ranging from assistive communication to entertainment. In this paper, we present RT-VC, a zero-shot real-time voice conversion system that delivers ultra-low latency and high-quality performance. Our approach leverages an articulatory feature space to naturally disentangle content and speaker characteristics, facilitating more robust and interpretable voice transformations. Additionally, the integration of differentiable digital signal processing (DDSP) enables efficient vocoding directly from articulatory features, significantly reducing conversion latency.
